Speaker

Dian Gomes

A catalyst of the Sri Lankan apparel industry, Dian is the Chairman of Hela Clothing. He is a former Group Director of MAS Holdings (USD 1.7 billion Apparel Enterprise) and the former Managing Director/CEO of its largest business division, MAS Intimates. Having commenced his career at KPMG, he moved on to become the youngest General Manager of Fortune 500 listed company, May Corporation, prior to embarking on his 25-year career in apparel.

A Fellow Member of the Chartered Institute of Management Accountants (UK), the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (UK) and Certified Practicing Accountants (Australia), he received his Executive Education at Wharton, Ashridge and Harvard Business Schools. Dian was President of the Chartered Institute of Management Accountants (UK) – Sri Lanka Division in 2001/2002 and presently sits on the Boards of many private and government organizations.

His versatile interests apart from business saw him take over the Presidency of the Amateur Boxing Association of Sri Lanka from 2004-2009, during which he provided strategic leadership and direction to guide Sri Lanka to a significant standing in international boxing. Dian currently serves as the Vice President of the National Olympic Committee and as the President of the National Fencing Association. He also sits in the Women’s Commission in the Amateur International Boxing Association (AIBA). His commitment to the development of sports across the country is renowned in local and overseas forums. He has represented Sri Lanka at numerous international sporting events in the capacity of Chef de Mission, including the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games. In 2013, he became the first Sri Lankan to be appointed as one of the 14 Members of the Commonwealth Advisory Body on Sport (CABOS), the key sports policy advisory body to the Commonwealth Secretariat. He was awarded the African Sports Management and Leadership Award in 2014 for his Outstanding Individual Contribution to Sports.

Dian was awarded Business Leader of the Year 2003 at the CIMA-Janashakthi Pinnacle Awards and was ‘Highly Commended’ by CIMA-UK at the Business Leader Awards in London in 2004. His unparalleled leadership was recognized, even after 10 years, when he was named as the Business icon of the year 2012 at the CIMA Leadership awards. Recognized as one of Sri Lanka’s ‘Male Icons’ by the Sri Lanka Institute of Marketing in 2007, Dian was awarded the ‘Brand Leadership Award’ under the Individual Category, in Mumbai, by the World Brand Congress in 2009.

He has presented numerous papers on organizational behavior, and has lectured widely on Leadership and Motivation at a variety of Sri Lankan and International forums, including Imperial College Business School London and Said Business School, University of Oxford, UK. His work in endorsing and promoting ethical business practices gained him an invitation as one of the fifty influential speakers from across the globe as a panelist at the Symposium of Gender Inequality in Emerging Markets, at Green Templeton College, University of Oxford, January 2013.

With a need to cater to Sri Lankan artists and art lovers alike in 2004 Dian envisioned a creative haven, bringing Gandhara, a life-style store, to life. Dian’s keen eye on spotting talent in the sporting and business arenas also sparks when it comes to art and crafts, supporting talented local artists and artisans through the platform of Gandhara.
