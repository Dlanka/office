About Us

A New Beginning

We can help smoothen and ease your transition into your new career and role and get you to start right so that you end right.

The graduating student entering the workforce for the first time, the professional-in-transit/in-between jobs, the aspirant entrepreneur, the retiring employee…they all have one thing in common – they will soon be embarking on new careers and assuming new roles.

Ill-prepared and unequipped for the change, many of them will go through a very difficult and painful period of adjusting to the new phase of life they are starting.

Preparing for such a game-changing event as embarking on a new career or assuming a new role in life begins with the need for one to prepare himself mentally for the new challenges that he will be facing. A new mindset with a new value system and new paradigms will have to be quickly forged to replace the old.

Starting a new job or career, or assuming a new role in life, naturally also requires one to possess new skills and competencies. These must be quickly acquired and mastered.

Learning new skills and competencies by itself is normally not a major problem. The crucial need is first to de-learn what has become irrelevant - to discard old habits and behavior patterns.

Start Right

Much has been talked about the academic institutions producing students who are ill-equipped to face the “real” world, the world of work and business.

As such, most of the leaving or graduating students go through a very painful period of adjusting to their new roles as responsible working adults. Learning through trial and error, some succeed while others fail to realize their full potential and capabilities.

The problem is compounded by the rapidly changing work environment posing an even greater challenge for the leaving or graduating students.

Stay Relevant

Managers today must realize that they can no longer afford to be merely technocrats or administrators placed in their positions to ensure that work gets done, and that the organization's policies and rules and procedures are not breached

They are now expected to earn their keep by contributing to the results of the organization. They must be highly market and business driven, able to look at important issues from a commercial perspective and make decisions that are not only technically correct but also make good business sense. They must constantly be in touch with their environment and sensitive to changes and developments taking place. They must anticipate threats to them and be able to identify and seek business opportunities to capitalize on.

These are among the many new challenges faced by managers today - challenges they must overcome to stay ahead of the curve.

Look Ahead

Retrenchment, redundancy, downsizing, rightsizing - it does not matter what the term used is, you have lost your job. The separation benefits (if any), more often than not, do not go far enough to alleviate your emotional trauma and financial hardships that follow.

Caring and socially responsible organizations can and should do more for the staff that they have no choice but to let go. How? They can provide outplacement help to the staff to enable them to cope better with their job loss and to find another job or form of livelihood in the shortest possible time.

Stay Active

Today's fifty or sixty-something are much healthier and better informed than those of yesteryears. Life expectancy has also increased significantly, and statistics show that these people have many more years of productive living and contribution ahead of them.

Armed with life's experiences, accumulated work skills and well-weathered wisdom, retires may yet find the new phase of life they are embarking on to be their most productive and satisfying.
