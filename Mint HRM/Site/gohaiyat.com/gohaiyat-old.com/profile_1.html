Profile

I am an entrepreneur, an educator, a trainer, a storyteller, and an artist because I have taken a journey through many years of trials and errors and because of the many people who pushed me to define my dreams and reach them. While my career in banking seemed accidental at the start, I grew to realize that I can choose to build the path I want to walk. As an entrepreneur and a mentor, I advocate for agility at work and in business as a means to succeed in an ever-changing environment. We need to be highly marketable and business driven; to be able to look at important issues from a commercial perspective; and make decisions that are not only technically correct, but also make good business sense.

Goh Ai Yat, a former senior banker, is the Managing Consultant of SBF Group of consulting companies.

Ai Yat regularly conducts entrepreneurial and development programmes. She trains organisational employees to adopt entrepreneurial thinking in preparation for increasingly competitive business landscapes, and individuals who are aspiring entrepreneurs.

She is privileged to be recognized as the first principal trainer in Asia for The Back of the Napkin™ - Solving Problems with Pictures. The Back of the Napkin was voted the Best Innovation Book of 2008 by BusinessWeek and Fast Company. It was Amazon’s fifth best selling business book in 2008 and a best seller in China, South Korea, and Japan.

Ai Yat also developed and conducts the programmes: Starting Your Own Business, The Self-Employed Training Workshops, The Entrepreneurial Manager, The Entrepreneurial Employee, Thriving On Challenges, Visual Thinking, Creativity & Innovation - The Rules Have Changed!, and Networking & Relationship Management.

Ai Yat is also the principal facilitator for the CAAT (Credit Assess-Analyst Training) – a 35-day Simulated Internship Scheme for undergraduates and Credit Guarantee Corporation’s ACE (Advance Credit Enhancer) Programme, an internal certification training programme for the organization’s credit personnel.

Her clients include multi-national companies, government agencies, as well as educational and financial institutions in Singapore, Malaysia, Bangladesh, Brunei, China, Hong Kong, India, Indonesia, Macau, Philippines, and Sri Lanka.

Besides training, Ai Yat is also actively involved in advising and helping business people in identifying and structuring their financing needs, preparation of feasibility reports, development of new markets, and business tie-ups. She currently teaches Entrepreneurship for the RMIT University degree programme at SIM Global Education, and Entrepreneurship and Strategic Finance Issues for an Australian MBA program.
